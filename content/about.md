I design and build web, GIS, and AI-assisted systems for data-driven projects, public institutions, cultural organizations, and interactive installations.

My strongest area of specialization is geospatial software: Web GIS platforms, spatial data pipelines, MapServer, GeoServer, PostGIS, QGIS, and interactive map interfaces.

I work as an external consultant and senior software developer at the Joint Research Centre (JRC) in Ispra for the Copernicus Emergency Management Service, E1 Floods section. I contribute to the development and maintenance of EFAS and GloFAS websites and other web applications, collaborating with ECMWF-hosted repositories and workflows.

Alongside GIS systems, I work on AI-based applications and high-impact user interfaces. I integrate large language models and AI services into practical tools, with attention to usability, visual quality, and clear interaction design.

I am particularly interested in digital twin and decision-support interfaces where risk managers can explore scenarios, compare alternatives, and run what-if simulations through intuitive, map-based, and visually rich environments.

I usually work across the full lifecycle of a project: requirements analysis, architecture, backend and frontend development, data modeling, deployment, remote monitoring, and long-term maintenance.
